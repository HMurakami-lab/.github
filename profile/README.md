## About The Lab

Murakami Lab investigates how cells control **meiotic recombination**: the
programmed formation and repair of DNA double-strand breaks that allows
chromosomes to recombine and segregate accurately during gamete formation.

We use **yeast meiosis** as a model system to understand how chromosome-axis
proteins and meiotic DNA break factors are positioned and regulated along
chromosomes.

> **Core question:** how do chromosome-axis proteins help shape where and when
> meiotic DNA double-strand breaks form?

---

## Research Themes

| Theme | What we study |
| --- | --- |
| Meiotic recombination initiation | How programmed DNA double-strand breaks are formed and controlled |
| Chromosome-axis regulation | Roles of factors including Hop1 and Red1 in meiotic chromosome organization |
| Recombination landscape control | How cells direct recombination to appropriate chromosomal regions |
| Yeast molecular genetics | Experimental systems for dissecting meiotic chromosome biology |
| Reproducible genomics | Pipelines for sequencing-based experiments, quality control, and data sharing |

---

## In The Lab And At The Terminal

- Calibrated ChIP-seq and genome-wide chromatin occupancy analysis
- S1-seq and sequencing workflows for mapping meiotic DNA double-strand breaks
- Yeast genetics, molecular biology, protein analysis, and chromosome assays
- R, Python, Bash, Linux, and HPC workflows for reproducible analysis
- Publication-linked scripts, documentation, and data-processing records
